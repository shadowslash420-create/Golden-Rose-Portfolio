For a luxury, high-end bakery named Golden Rose Bakes that specializes in wedding sweets and traditional/modern pastries, the color palette needs to feel appetizing, premium, and romantic.The goal is to avoid looking like a generic fast-food app (which uses aggressive reds and bright yellows) and instead look like a high-end boutique.Here is the perfect production-ready color strategy, broken down by their UI roles and design psychology:The Master Color Palette1. The Core Brand Colors (60% of the UI)These colors dominate the background and overall layout to establish a clean, elegant canvas that makes the photography of the sweets pop.Alabaster Cream (#FFFDFB): A soft, warm, off-white background. Pure white looks too clinical and digital; a creamy white feels organic, like flour, powdered sugar, and high-end baking parchment.Soft Rose Velvet (#FFF1F2 to #FFE4E6): Used for card backgrounds, secondary tags, and subtle structural dividers. It brings in the "Rose" aspect of the brand without overwhelming the user's eyes.2. The Identity Accents (30% of the UI)These colors are used for prominent typography, headings, organic floral frames, and key UI elements.Blush Rose Pink (#E11D48 to #FB7185): The primary brand color. It represents romance, weddings, and sweetness. It should be used on active states, category highlights, and floral decorative icons.Muted Sage/Olive Green (#6B7280 or #A7F3D0 tints): The perfect complementary color for roses. Using a soft botanical green for secondary elements (like "In Stock" tags or natural ingredients) anchors the floral theme beautifully.3. The Premium & Action Colors (10% of the UI)Reserved strictly for high-priority elements like primary call-to-action buttons ("Submit Inquiry", "Book Date") and price tags.Deep Roasted Amber/Espresso (#451A03 or #292524): Instead of using harsh pure black (#000000) for text, use a very dark, rich espresso brown or deep charcoal. It feels much softer, premium, and mimics roasted coffee or dark chocolate.Soft Champagne Gold (#D97706 / Metallic gradients): Used sparingly for badges, premium tiers ("Wedding Cakes"), or subtle borders to honor the "Golden" in the name without looking tacky or gaudy.Color Mapping Reference TableUI ComponentColor RoleTailwind CSS Class SuggestionMain BackgroundWarm Ivory / Alabasterbg-[#FFFDFB]Primary Text / HeadingsDeep Espresso Browntext-amber-950 or text-stone-900Action Buttons (CTA)Deep Luxury Roastbg-gradient-to-l from-amber-950 to-stone-900Floral Frames & AccentsSoft Rose / Blushtext-rose-300 and bg-rose-50Status Tags (e.g., Wedding)Vibrant Rosebg-rose-600 text-white
